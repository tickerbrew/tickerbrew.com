Amazon Engineers Slam AI Spending Amid Layoffs

Amazon is facing significant internal backlash from its engineering workforce regarding the company's ambitious $200 billion allocation towards AI infrastructure this year. This substantial investment comes at a time when Amazon has implemented widespread layoffs, impacting over 30,000 corporate employees since October. The dissent was publicly voiced during a Seattle City Council meeting, where local officials subsequently passed a proposal to impose a one-year moratorium on new mega data center developments within the city.

This situation underscores a growing tension between the rapid pursuit of AI advancements and the welfare of the existing workforce. While Amazon aims to solidify its position in the AI market, the optics of massive AI spending alongside job cuts have drawn criticism. The engineers' concerns highlight potential misalignment in corporate priorities, prompting questions about the balance between technological innovation and employee stability.

The Seattle City Council's decision to limit data center expansion reflects a broader trend of local governments scrutinizing the environmental and social impact of large tech operations. This regulatory move could set a precedent for other municipalities, potentially complicating future growth plans for tech giants dependent on extensive data center infrastructure to power their AI initiatives.

Coinbase Opens SpaceX Pre-IPO Futures Market

Coinbase has introduced an innovative pre-IPO perpetual futures product, providing investors with an unprecedented opportunity to gain early exposure to highly anticipated private companies, starting with SpaceX. This new offering, accessible via Coinbase Advanced, facilitates around-the-clock trading of USDC-settled perpetual futures. Investors can now leverage up to 5x on SpaceX, a company reportedly valued at an astounding $1.8 trillion.

The launch of this product signifies a notable evolution in how retail investors can access private market valuations. Historically, participation in such high-growth private ventures was largely restricted to institutional investors and accredited individuals. Coinbase's move effectively democratizes this access, allowing a broader range of investors to speculate on the future value of private entities before their public debut.

This development is not just about SpaceX; it could establish a new paradigm for the financial industry. By offering perpetual futures on private companies, Coinbase is blurring the traditional lines between public and private market investing. This could prompt other exchanges to explore similar products, potentially leading to a more integrated and accessible global market for both established and emerging companies, profoundly influencing AI market shifts and other high-growth sectors.

CrowdStrike Beats Estimates, Stock Still Falls 10%

Despite narrowly surpassing its earnings estimates, cybersecurity firm CrowdStrike experienced a significant 10% drop in its stock price following the announcement. This reaction highlights the extreme sensitivity of the market to perceived threats and future growth prospects, even when current financial performance is strong. CrowdStrike has seen considerable volatility throughout 2026, with its stock plummeting to $342.77 in February before staging a robust recovery to recent highs of $677.50.

The primary factor weighing on investor sentiment appears to be the looming threat of competition from next-generation AI models. Specifically, fears surrounding Anthropic's Claude Code Security, an advanced AI model designed for code security, have cast a shadow over CrowdStrike's future market position. Investors are concerned that new AI-powered solutions could disrupt the traditional cybersecurity landscape, potentially eroding CrowdStrike's competitive edge.

This market response underscores a critical aspect of the current investment climate: in the era of rapid AI market shifts, even a company delivering solid financial results may not be immune to investor skepticism if there are perceived long-term competitive challenges from emerging AI technologies. It signals that the market is prioritizing future innovation and competitive moats over past performance, especially in sectors highly susceptible to AI disruption.

Under the Radar

Broadcom's AI Boom Bets Clouded by Revenue Miss

Broadcom shares experienced a significant decline, sinking 12% in premarket trading, after the company reported quarterly revenue that fell short of analyst expectations. This disappointment could potentially wipe over $285 billion from its market capitalization, based on its current price of $418.83 per share. The miss signals a challenging environment even for companies positioned within the booming AI sector.

The revenue shortfall highlights the intensifying competition within the AI chip market, a critical component of the broader AI market shifts. Broadcom, a major player in this space, is locked in a fierce battle with industry giants like Nvidia, which currently dominates the high-performance AI chip segment. Investors are keenly watching how companies navigate this competitive landscape, and any stumble can be heavily penalized.

This event serves as a stark reminder that participation in the AI boom does not guarantee success without flawless execution. Even with strong strategic bets on AI, companies must meet or exceed market expectations to maintain investor confidence. The market is becoming increasingly discerning, demanding not just exposure to AI, but concrete results and a clear competitive advantage in this rapidly evolving technological frontier.
